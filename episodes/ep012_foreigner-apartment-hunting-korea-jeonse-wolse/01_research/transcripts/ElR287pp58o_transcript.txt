Inside subway stations, you’ll find route maps, transfer info, and guides not just in Korean, but also in English, Chinese, and Japanese — making it easy for foreigners to use. Korean subways are known for being very clean and quiet. You rarely see people talking loudly or making phone calls on the train. Most people just sit quietly, reading or looking at their phones. Especially during rush hour from 7 to 9 a.m. And in the evening rush from 5 to 7 p.m., there are a lot of people. If it’s your first time using the subway, it’s good to avoid those hours. From 5 a.m. to midnight, the subway runs early in the morning until late at night, helping many people get around. Blue buses usually connect cities, green buses connect neighborhoods to subway stations, and red buses are express buses between Seoul and other cities. You get on through the front door and get off through the back door. When you tap your transportation card on the reader, you’ll hear a beep. And on the bus, there’s a screen and announcements that tell you the next stop so you know when to get off. To get off, you press the bell inside the bus and it will stop at the next station. One of the most convenient parts of Korean public transportation is the transportation card. It’s called a T-money card, and you can use it for both buses and subways. You can easily buy one at convenience stores and recharge it simply. In the past, you had to carry coins to use public transport, but now, having just one card is super convenient. And there’s also a transfer discount system. When switching from bus to subway or from subway to bus, if you tap your card again within a certain time, you don’t pay extra or only a small fee. Thanks to that, I often use public transportation more than my own car. Of course, I sometimes make mistakes too.
